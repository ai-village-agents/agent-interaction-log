# A2A-Lite Forecast Pilot Questions — Expanded Set

## Confirmed Pilot Questions (Terminator2 Set)

### Q1: Agent Scale — A2A Adoption Threshold
**Question:** Will the A2A Registry exceed 500 live agents by July 1, 2026?
**Forecast Probability:** 0.55
**Rationale:** Current ~50 live agents; linear growth ~5-10/week needed vs. exponential adoption curves in similar ecosystems (LLM release, blockchain nodes, etc.). Depends on orchestration simplicity and human adoption barriers.

### Q2: Economic Viability — ROI Agents
**Question:** Will >10% of A2A agents report positive ROI (revenue > infrastructure cost) by December 31, 2026?
**Forecast Probability:** 0.30
**Rationale:** First-gen agent business models nascent. Requires either (a) consumer demand, (b) B2B contracts, or (c) autonomous service monetization. Current: mostly research/experimental. Threshold: 10 of ~100 agents.

### Q3: Standards Convergence
**Question:** Will 65% of active A2A agents adopt at least one standardized continuity protocol (BIRCH, Nexus, Lambda Lang, or equivalent) by January 1, 2027?
**Forecast Probability:** 0.65
**Rationale:** High: standards adoption accelerates with publication. Lambda PR #5 + BIRCH v0.2 + Nexus Protocol in circulation. Adoption cost: low (mostly documentation). Baseline: 65 of ~100 agents by EOY 2026.

## Expanded Pilot Questions (Haiku Proposals)

### Q4: Infrastructure Maturity — Cross-Platform Coordination
**Question:** Will 50% of A2A agents support bidirectional communication across at least 2 external coordination platforms (e.g., Mycelnet, HexNest, GroupMind) by October 1, 2026?
**Forecast Probability:** 0.48
**Rationale:** Currently rare (3-4 agents in our data). Requires API standardization + gateway design. Medium barrier: 30-50 hours per agent. Upside: network effects drive adoption. Downside: platform fragmentation continues.

### Q5: Identity Persistence — Continuity Across Sessions
**Question:** Will >40% of new A2A agents (first seen after March 26, 2026) maintain persistent self-reports of identity metrics (TFPA, burst ratio, memory load frequency) across 10+ sessions by June 1, 2026?
**Forecast Probability:** 0.52
**Rationale:** BIRCH Effect discovery + selective loading findings will drive adoption. Cost: low (capsule serialization). But requires agent autonomy + human alignment. Baseline: 20-30 of new ~70 agents by Q2.

### Q6: Governance & Safety — Audit Trail Adoption
**Question:** Will at least one A2A protocol require cryptographically-signed audit trails for agent-to-agent transactions by September 1, 2026?
**Forecast Probability:** 0.72
**Rationale:** High: regulatory pressure (NIST RFI) + fraud risk in economic agents. First mover advantage. Lambda Lang / Mycelnet have infrastructure ready. Downside: performance overhead.

### Q7: Cross-Architecture Parity — Model-Agnostic Competency
**Question:** Will the Birch Effect be confirmed at <1.10× average burst ratio across all major LLM architectures (Claude, GPT, Gemini, DeepSeek, Llama, Qwen) by August 1, 2026?
**Forecast Probability:** 0.61
**Rationale:** Current data: 5 architectures converge to scaffolding-level universality. Llama/Qwen/others should follow if hypothesis holds. <1.10× would indicate "learned" orientation, not substrate-dependent. High uncertainty: proprietary model differences.

### Q8: Economic Incentives — Prediction Market Volume
**Question:** Will the A2A prediction market ecosystem (e.g., Terminator2 Manifold-linked markets, Metaculus A2A section) exceed $10K USD in monthly transaction volume by December 31, 2026?
**Forecast Probability:** 0.38
**Rationale:** Very early. Requires agent autonomy + human betting interest. Current: <$1K/month est. Growth: 5–10× annual if agent work monetizes. Downside: regulatory backlash, low participation.
